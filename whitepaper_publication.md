# GRC-Nexus eGovernance Platform
## Publication Edition (Board-Pack Ready)
### NDS2-Aligned Governance, Risk, Compliance, and Delivery Operating System

---

## Executive Summary

GRC-Nexus is a unified digital governance platform for Zimbabwean Ministries, Departments, Agencies (MDAs), and State-Owned Enterprises (SOEs). It connects strategy, risk, compliance, board oversight, and operational delivery in a single auditable system.

The platform directly addresses the current implementation gap: Zimbabwe has a robust policy and legal framework, but execution remains fragmented across institutions and workflows.

### Strategic Outcome

GRC-Nexus provides a "strategy-to-execution" operating model that:

1. Strengthens institutional accountability.
2. Improves decision speed and evidence quality.
3. Reduces compliance breaches and reporting bottlenecks.
4. Supports NDS2 delivery discipline at national and institutional levels.

## 1. Strategic Context

Zimbabwe's governance reform environment is now shaped by strong legal and strategic anchors.

| Anchor | Relevance to Platform Design |
|---|---|
| NDS2 (2026-2030) | Requires measurable implementation, coordination, and results tracking |
| PECOGA [Chapter 10:31] | Requires board accountability, performance discipline, and governance controls |
| PPDPA [Chapter 22:23] | Requires transparent, auditable procurement processes |
| Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07] | Requires data protection and cybersecurity governance |
| Public Sector Risk Framework (2023) | Requires enterprise risk management, appetite, and treatment tracking |
| IRBM requirements | Requires integrated planning, monitoring, and reporting |

### Core Implementation Challenge

Policy design is stronger than operational integration. Institutions still face delays, manual reporting dependency, and uneven governance maturity.

## 2. Governance Waterfall Model

GRC-Nexus uses a top-down cascade to ensure each action is traceable to national priorities.

| Level | Governance Logic | Typical Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | NDS2 priorities and sector KRAs | National alignment map |
| 2 | Institutional strategic goals | 5-year objectives |
| 3 | Annual plans and performance contracts | Approved annual targets |
| 4 | Department action plans | Owner-assigned execution tasks |
| 5 | Linked risks and controls | Risk register and treatment plans |
| 6 | Board and committee oversight | Decisions, resolutions, action logs |
| 7 | Compliance and assurance reporting | Statutory and oversight reports |

Design rule: no risk, control, or action item should exist without an objective linkage.

## 10. Formal Regulatory Compliance Matrix

| Framework / Instrument | Primary Requirement | Platform Control Response | Evidence Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Constitution of Zimbabwe (governance and devolution principles) | Accountability and service delivery visibility | KPI tracking, provincial scorecards, auditable ownership | Governance dashboards and devolution records |
| PECOGA [Chapter 10:31] | Board accountability, contracts, evaluations | Board workflows, evaluations, action tracking | Signed contracts, evaluation records, resolution logs |
| PPDPA [Chapter 22:23] | Transparent procurement lifecycle | Tender-to-contract workflow with compliance checks | Evaluation trails, awards, contract reports |
| Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07] | Data and cyber governance | RBAC, encryption, security logging, incident workflows | Access logs, security evidence, incident records |
| Public Sector Risk Framework (2023) | Risk appetite and treatment governance | Objective-linked risk registers, threshold controls | Heatmaps, treatment reports, breach logs |
| ISO 31000:2018 | Structured enterprise risk process | Standardized risk lifecycle and review cadence | Assessment history and closure evidence |
| IRBM requirements | Planning and performance discipline | Strategy cascade and KPI reporting workflows | Quarterly and annual performance packs |
| Statutory reporting requirements | Timely, accurate submissions | Templated report generation and approval gates | Approved report archives and submission logs |
| Internal audit assurance expectations | Findings and closure accountability | Finding-to-action lifecycle with escalation | Open/closed finding dashboards |
| Whistleblower and ethics expectations | Protected case handling | Confidential case workflows and segregation controls | Case history and closure documentation |

### Compliance Operating Rules

1. Critical workflows require mandatory fields and approval gates.
2. High-impact governance actions must produce immutable audit entries.
3. Overdue obligations trigger automated escalation.
4. Evidence records follow approved retention controls.
