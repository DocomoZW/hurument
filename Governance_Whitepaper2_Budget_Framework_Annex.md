# Annex D: Implementation Budget Framework
## ZIGDP Costing and Funding Structure (Indicative)

**Version:** 2.0
**Date:** May 2026

---

## 1. Budgeting Principles

1. Phase-gated funding aligned to measurable deliverables.
2. Separate CAPEX and OPEX visibility.
3. Independent contingency reserve for high-impact risks.
4. Benefit-tracking to justify expansion decisions.

---

## 2. Cost Categories

1. Program mobilization and governance setup.
2. Platform software licensing/subscription.
3. Implementation services and integration development.
4. Infrastructure/hosting and security tooling.
5. Data migration and quality remediation.
6. Training, change management, and communications.
7. Independent assurance, audit, and testing.
8. Support, maintenance, and managed operations.

---

## 3. Phase-Based Budget Buckets

### Phase 1 (Months 1-4): Foundation

1. Program office setup.
2. Architecture and design.
3. Baseline integrations and identity services.

### Phase 2 (Months 5-10): Core Integrity Stack

1. Procurement module rollout.
2. Risk and compliance module rollout.
3. Audit workflow enablement.
4. Whistleblower MVP.

### Phase 3 (Months 11-16): Delivery Stack

1. Program/project tracking rollout.
2. Coordination workspace.
3. Provincial scorecards and dashboards.

### Phase 4 (Months 17-24): Scale

1. Additional entities onboarding.
2. Advanced analytics and optimization.
3. Hardening and sustained operations.

---

## 4. Indicative Percentage Allocation (Total Program)

1. Software/platform and licenses: 18-25%
2. Implementation and integration services: 30-40%
3. Security, assurance, and testing: 8-12%
4. Data migration and quality improvement: 8-12%
5. Training and change management: 8-10%
6. Program governance and PMO: 6-9%
7. Contingency reserve: 8-12%

---

## 5. Funding Sources (Potential Mix)

1. National budget appropriations.
2. Development partner co-financing.
3. Institution-specific contribution model for onboarding.
4. Performance-linked disbursement windows.

---

## 6. Financial Controls

1. Milestone acceptance prior to payment release.
2. Independent verification of high-value deliverables.
3. Monthly burn-rate, variance, and forecast reporting.
4. Quarterly budget rebaseline process with governance approval.

---

## 7. Benefit Realization Framework

Benefit categories:

1. Efficiency gains (time and processing reductions).
2. Control effectiveness gains (fewer compliance breaches).
3. Risk reduction gains (faster detection and mitigation).
4. Delivery quality gains (improved NDS2 execution metrics).

Tracking cadence:

1. Monthly operational indicators.
2. Quarterly benefit review.
3. Annual impact assessment.

---

## 8. Budget Risks and Safeguards

1. Scope expansion without funding -> enforce change control board.
2. Underestimated integration complexity -> maintain contingency reserve.
3. Vendor dependency concentration -> multi-lot procurement strategy.
4. Low adoption reducing value realization -> prioritize training investment.
