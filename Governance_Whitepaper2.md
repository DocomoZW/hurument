## Executive Summary

Zimbabwe's governance reform momentum is real and measurable. The policy architecture exists, digital procurement controls are active, and Enterprise Risk Management adoption is expanding. The central implementation gap is no longer strategy definition; it is integrated execution across ministries, parastatals, provinces, and local authorities.

Version 2 proposes a practical solution: a unified national platform that combines Governance, Risk, Compliance, procurement integrity, and NDS2 delivery management into a single operational system.

This whitepaper introduces the **Zimbabwe Integrated Governance and Development Platform (ZIGDP)** as a national digital operating model to:

- Improve public sector transparency and accountability.
- Institutionalize risk-led decision making.
- Reduce procurement and delivery leakages.
- Strengthen inter-agency coordination under OPC leadership.
- Track NDS2 outcomes in near real time.

## 2. Strategic Problem

The source notebooks consistently point to a common operating challenge:

1. Governance controls are improving but remain distributed across institutions.
2. Risk and compliance systems are being procured independently, with limited interoperability.
3. NDS2 delivery monitoring risks becoming fragmented if IRBM digitalization is delayed.
4. Devolution and cross-ministry coordination still face data and execution bottlenecks.

The result is policy-execution drag: institutions comply within silos while national outcomes require integrated coordination.

---

## 3. The Proposed National Platform

The proposed platform, ZIGDP, integrates nine capability domains:

1. Digital procurement and contract integrity.
2. Enterprise GRC (risk, audit, compliance, control testing).
3. Whistleblower and ethics case management.
4. NDS2 portfolio and performance delivery management.
5. Inter-ministerial coordination workspace.
6. Investment and project pipeline tracking.
7. Devolution and sub-national performance scorecards.
8. Policy and statutory compliance registry.
9. National analytics and executive decision dashboards.

---

## 4. Operating Outcomes

If implemented in staged releases, ZIGDP should deliver measurable public value:

1. Reduced procurement cycle times and compliance defects.
2. Faster detection and treatment of institutional risk events.
3. Stronger board and audit committee oversight quality.
4. Improved delivery reliability of NDS2 flagship projects.
5. Higher confidence for citizens, investors, and development partners.

---

## 5. Governance and Ownership Model

Recommended governance structure:

1. **National Steering Committee**
   - Sponsorship and policy direction.
   - Prioritization and cross-ministry escalation.

2. **Platform Management Office (PMO)**
   - Product roadmap, release governance, architecture guardrails.
   - Service management, data quality assurance, adoption tracking.

3. **Institution Product Owners**
   - Workflow configuration and KPI ownership per ministry/entity.

4. **Independent Assurance Layer**
   - Internal audit, Auditor-General, and oversight institutions with controlled evidence access.

---

## 6. Delivery Roadmap (24 Months)

1. **Phase 1 (Months 1-4): Foundation**
   - Program mobilization, architecture baseline, pilot scope, legal and data governance alignment.

2. **Phase 2 (Months 5-10): Core Integrity Stack**
   - Procurement integrity, risk registers, compliance obligations, audit workflows, whistleblower MVP.

3. **Phase 3 (Months 11-16): NDS2 Delivery Stack**
   - Portfolio tracking, inter-ministerial planning, provincial scorecards, executive dashboards.

4. **Phase 4 (Months 17-24): National Scale and Optimization**
   - Expansion to additional entities, advanced analytics, predictive indicators, continuous controls testing.

---

## 7. Success Metrics

Program-level KPIs:

1. Procurement processing time reduction.
2. On-time closure rate for audit and compliance findings.
3. High-risk event detection-to-response time.
4. Percentage of NDS2 projects on schedule and within budget.
5. Timeliness and completeness of devolution reporting.
6. Platform active usage rate across enrolled institutions.
