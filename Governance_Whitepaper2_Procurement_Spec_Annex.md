# Annex C: Procurement Specification Outline
## ZIGDP Implementation Procurement Pack

**Version:** 2.0
**Date:** May 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

Provide a standard procurement specification framework for acquiring implementation services and platform components for ZIGDP.

---

## 2. Procurement Lots (Recommended)

1. Lot 1: Platform core implementation and configuration.
2. Lot 2: Data integration and interoperability services.
3. Lot 3: Cybersecurity, assurance, and control testing.
4. Lot 4: Training, change management, and adoption support.
5. Lot 5: Support and managed operations (optional).

---

## 3. Vendor Eligibility Requirements

Mandatory documents:

1. Valid business registration documents.
2. Current tax clearance certificates.
3. Social security compliance documentation.
4. Relevant regulator registration where applicable.
5. Signed conflict-of-interest and integrity declarations.

Mandatory capabilities:

1. Proven delivery of enterprise governance/risk/compliance platforms.
2. Experience with public sector or regulated environments.
3. Demonstrable cybersecurity and data protection competence.
4. Local implementation and knowledge transfer plan.

---

## 4. Technical Specification (Minimum)

1. API-first architecture and documented integration interfaces.
2. Configurable workflows for procurement, risk, audit, and compliance.
3. Role-based access controls and comprehensive audit logging.
4. Dashboard and reporting capabilities for executive and operational users.
5. Data migration and validation tooling.
6. High availability and backup/recovery design.

---

## 5. Deliverables by Vendor

1. Solution design documents and architecture artifacts.
2. Configured modules per agreed release scope.
3. Integration interfaces and test harnesses.
4. Security baseline and penetration test reports.
5. Training materials and user manuals.
6. Hypercare and stabilization support.

---

## 6. Service Levels (Illustrative)

1. Production availability: 99.5% monthly.
2. Priority-1 incident response: <= 1 hour.
3. Priority-1 restoration target: <= 4 hours.
4. Priority-2 restoration target: <= 1 business day.
5. Monthly service and security performance reporting.

---

## 7. Evaluation Model

Recommended weighted criteria:

1. Technical compliance and architecture fit: 35%.
2. Relevant implementation experience and references: 25%.
3. Security and compliance approach: 15%.
4. Delivery plan and team quality: 15%.
5. Financial proposal and value for money: 10%.

---

## 8. Commercial Structure

1. Milestone-based payments tied to accepted deliverables.
2. Retention percentage until end of stabilization period.
3. Clear change request and rate card mechanisms.
4. Penalties for critical SLA breaches and prolonged delays.

---

## 9. Contractual Clauses (Critical)

1. Data ownership retained by Government of Zimbabwe institutions.
2. Data residency and sovereignty obligations.
3. Confidentiality and whistleblower data protection obligations.
4. Source code escrow or transition assistance for continuity.
5. Mandatory knowledge transfer to designated local teams.

---

## 10. Implementation Governance Requirements

1. Joint steering committee and weekly PMO cadence.
2. Stage-gate approvals per release.
3. Independent quality assurance checkpoints.
4. Formal go-live readiness criteria and sign-off.
